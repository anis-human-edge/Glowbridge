# **2\) Executive Summary**

We are building an end-to-end web application that helps:

* **traction-backed skincare brands** get discovered in new markets  
* **rising skincare creators** get access, status, opportunities, and community  
* **real users** discover products through trusted creator-led interaction  
* **the platform** monetize supplier-side access, activation, insight, and commerce pathways

The business starts in skincare under the wider beauty domain.

The operating model is:

* **global digital discovery events**  
* **London-first physical trust events**  
* **supplier-first monetization**  
* **creator-led discovery and experience first**  
* **reach first, then trust / engagement / consistency / category fit**  
* **social traction as the first motion signal for brand selection**

The product is not just a marketplace and not just a landing page.  
It is a **curated discovery operating system**.

---

# **3\) Product Vision**

Build the default global launch-and-discovery platform for emerging skincare brands and rising skincare creators, starting with discovery from high-growth Asian skincare ecosystems and compounding trust through recurring digital and physical rituals.

---

# **4\) Product Thesis**

This product wins if it does five things better than generic influencer platforms, event businesses, and beauty directories:

1. **Finds interesting brands before the mainstream notices**  
2. **Filters those brands through traction, category fit, and credibility**  
3. **Matches them with creators who have reach plus trust potential**  
4. **Turns repeated events into community, status, and proof loops**  
5. **Creates monetizable supplier value without destroying curation trust**

# **5\) The Core Problem**

## **5.1 Brand-side problem**

Traction-backed skincare brands from high-growth ecosystems struggle to enter new markets because:

* discovery is fragmented  
* creator selection is messy  
* local trust is hard to manufacture  
* events are expensive and inefficient  
* exposure without context does not convert

## **5.2 Creator-side problem**

Rising skincare creators struggle because:

* they get random low-quality sponsorship offers  
* they lack structured access to interesting new brands  
* there are few high-quality recurring category communities  
* there is no real progression from creator to reviewer to host to partner

## **5.3 Market problem**

Consumers are drowning in noise. Discovery is abundant. Trusted discovery is scarce.
